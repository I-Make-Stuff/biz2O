# Operating Agreement – Outline (LLC)

This document outlines the structure and intent of the Operating Agreement
for the Company (the “Company”).

The Operating Agreement is designed to:
- be readable by non-lawyers
- allow easy collaboration and participation
- support partial ownership without complexity
- separate ownership, incentives, and allocation
- evolve cleanly into a corporation

This outline is not legal advice.
It is a structural blueprint for drafting the final agreement.

---

## ARTICLE I — FORMATION & PURPOSE

### 1.1 Formation
- The Company is formed as a Limited Liability Company (LLC)
- Jurisdiction: [State]
- Formation date: [Date]

### 1.2 Purpose
The Company may engage in any lawful business activity, including but not limited to:
- product development
- manufacturing
- services
- research
- licensing
- collaboration with individuals and other businesses

The Company is designed to support open collaboration and evolving participation.

---

## ARTICLE II — DEFINITIONS

Key defined terms (plain language preferred):

- **Member** – a legal owner of the Company
- **Managing Authority** – the person or body with executive authority
- **Equity Units** – legal ownership interests
- **Allocation Credits** – internal distribution-weighting units
- **Reward Tokens** – internal incentive units
- **Directive Pools** – constitutionally defined allocation categories
- **Steward** – a delegated authority for a specific pool or function

Definitions must clearly state what something *is not* as well as what it is.

---

## ARTICLE III — MEMBERSHIP & OWNERSHIP

### 3.1 Initial Member
- The Company begins as a single-member LLC
- The initial Member holds 100% of Equity Units at formation

### 3.2 Additional Members
- New Members may be admitted by Managing Authority approval
- Membership may be fractional
- Ownership does not require operational involvement

### 3.3 Nature of Ownership
Ownership:
- conveys economic rights as defined herein
- does not automatically convey authority or employment
- may be subject to vesting, buyback, or restriction

This allows people to “own part of the company” without complexity.

---

## ARTICLE IV — EQUITY UNITS

### 4.1 Equity Units Defined
- Equity Units represent legal ownership
- Equity Units are securities
- Equity Units are governed by this Agreement

### 4.2 Issuance
- Equity Units may be issued for:
  - capital contribution
  - conversion events
  - approved grants
- Issuance requires Managing Authority approval

### 4.3 Vesting & Buyback
- Units may vest over time or milestones
- Unvested units may be repurchased upon exit
- Terms are specified per grant

---

## ARTICLE V — MANAGEMENT & AUTHORITY

### 5.1 Managing Authority
- The Company is manager-managed
- Initially, the Founder is the Managing Authority

Managing Authority responsibilities include:
- enforcing this Agreement
- appointing stewards
- approving distributions
- interpreting ambiguous provisions

### 5.2 Delegation
- Authority may be delegated in writing
- Delegation is revocable
- Delegation does not transfer ownership

---

## ARTICLE VI — DIRECTIVE POOLS & ALLOCATION MODEL

### 6.1 Allocation Directives
The Company adopts permanent allocation directives:

| Directive | Target Allocation |
|---------|------------------|
| Financial Assistance | 30% |
| Labor & Operations | 50% |
| Founder Allocation | 10% |
| Planning & Strategy | 10% |

These percentages govern **value distribution**, not ownership.

### 6.2 Allocation Credits
- Allocation Credits are internal accounting units
- They are non-transferable
- They do not represent property or equity
- They determine proportional participation within a pool

---

## ARTICLE VII — INCENTIVES & TASK-BASED CONTRIBUTION

### 7.1 Open Contribution Model
- The Company may offer tasks or contribution opportunities
- Participation is voluntary
- No reward is guaranteed

### 7.2 Reward Tokens
- Reward Tokens may be issued for validated contributions
- Reward Tokens are non-transferable
- Reward Tokens do not guarantee profit or ownership

This allows people to work with the Company easily without contracts up front.

---

## ARTICLE VIII — DISTRIBUTIONS

### 8.1 Discretionary Distributions
- Distributions are made at the discretion of Managing Authority
- No Member is entitled to automatic distributions

### 8.2 Distribution Waterfall
If distributions occur:
1. Distributable value is determined
2. Allocation Directives are applied
3. Allocation Credits determine internal splits
4. Form of payout is approved

---

## ARTICLE IX — OBLIGATIONS & CONTRACTUAL WORK

### 9.1 Obligations
- Some roles or relationships may include obligations
- Obligations are governed by separate agreements
- Failure to perform obligations does not create debt unless specified

### 9.2 Independent Status
Participation does not automatically create:
- employment
- partnership
- fiduciary duty

---

## ARTICLE X — TRANSFERS & RESTRICTIONS

### 10.1 Transfer Restrictions
- Equity Units may not be transferred without approval
- Right of first refusal applies

### 10.2 No Secondary Market
- No public trading of Equity Units
- No expectation of liquidity

---

## ARTICLE XI — RECORDS, LEDGERS & TECHNOLOGY

### 11.1 Recordkeeping
The Company may use:
- traditional accounting systems
- internal ledgers
- blockchain-based audit systems

Blockchain systems:
- record events
- do not define legal meaning

---

## ARTICLE XII — LIABILITY & INDEMNIFICATION

### 12.1 Limited Liability
Members are not personally liable for Company obligations.

### 12.2 Indemnification
The Company may indemnify:
- Managing Authority
- Stewards
- Agents

Except in cases of fraud or willful misconduct.

---

## ARTICLE XIII — AMENDMENTS & EVOLUTION

### 13.1 Amendments
- Ordinary amendments require Managing Authority approval
- Constitutional changes require supermajority approval

### 13.2 Evolution
The Company is expected to evolve.
Structures may change provided:
- allocation principles remain intact
- rights are not retroactively removed

---

## ARTICLE XIV — CONVERSION TO CORPORATION

### 14.1 Authority to Convert
- Managing Authority may approve conversion
- Members will receive corresponding equity

### 14.2 Continuity
Upon conversion:
- Equity Units convert to shares
- Allocation rules carry forward
- Incentive history remains valid

---

## ARTICLE XV — DISSOLUTION

### 15.1 Dissolution Events
- Member decision
- Legal requirement
- Sale or merger

### 15.2 Winding Up
Assets distributed according to:
- obligations
- allocation directives
- ownership rights

---

## ARTICLE XVI — GENERAL PROVISIONS

- Governing law
- Severability
- Notices
- Entire agreement clause

---

## DESIGN INTENT (NON-BINDING)

This Agreement is intentionally:
- modular
- human-readable
- collaboration-friendly
- evolution-ready

It is designed to make it easy to:
- work with the Company
- contribute value
- own part of the Company
without forcing early rigidity.
