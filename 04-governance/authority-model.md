# Authority Model

This document defines how authority is structured, delegated, constrained,
and audited within the organization.

Authority is intentionally separated from:
- Ownership
- Economic allocation
- Incentive rewards

This separation is foundational and non-optional.

---

## 1. Core Principle

Authority determines:
- Who may act
- Within what scope
- Under what constraints

Authority does NOT automatically entitle the holder to:
- Ownership
- Profit
- Allocation
- Permanence

---

## 2. Authority Layers

The organization recognizes four distinct layers of authority:

1. Constitutional Authority
2. Managing Authority
3. Directive Steward Authority
4. Operational Authority

Each layer is subordinate to the one above it.

---

## 3. Constitutional Authority (Highest Layer)

### 3.1 Definition

Constitutional Authority is the power to define or amend:
- Allocation Directives
- Governance structure
- Amendment rules
- Existence of internal units (credits, tokens, equity)

This authority is embedded in:
- LLC Operating Agreement (initially)
- Corporate Charter and Bylaws (post-conversion)

---

### 3.2 Scope

Constitutional Authority may:
- Create or dissolve directive pools
- Set or lock allocation percentages
- Define supermajority thresholds
- Authorize conversion of entity type

Constitutional Authority may NOT:
- Be exercised casually
- Be delegated to operational roles
- Be overridden by technical systems

---

### 3.3 Amendment Threshold

Changes to constitutional elements require:
- ≥90% approval of Constitutional Authority holders
- Written justification
- Cooling-off period (recommended minimum: 30 days)

---

## 4. Managing Authority

### 4.1 Definition

The Managing Authority is the supreme executive authority
responsible for enforcing the constitution and ensuring continuity.

Initially:
- The Founder is the sole Managing Authority.

---

### 4.2 Powers

Managing Authority may:
- Interpret constitutional rules
- Appoint and remove Directive Stewards
- Approve or halt distributions
- Determine distributable value
- Delegate limited authority

Managing Authority may NOT:
- Alter directive percentages unilaterally
- Bypass distribution rules
- Convert allocation credits into ownership

---

### 4.3 Fiduciary Obligation

The Managing Authority owes fiduciary duty to:
- The organization as a whole
- The constitutional directives
- Long-term system integrity

Failure to uphold fiduciary duty is grounds for removal
via constitutional process.

---

## 5. Directive Steward Authority

### 5.1 Definition

Directive Stewards are delegated authorities responsible for
allocating Allocation Credits within a specific directive pool.

Each pool may have at most one Steward at a time.

---

### 5.2 Steward Assignments

| Directive Pool | Steward Role |
|---------------|--------------|
| Financial Assistance | Finance Steward |
| Labor / Operations | Operations Steward |
| Founder Pool | Non-delegable |
| Planning / Strategy | Planning Steward |

---

### 5.3 Scope of Authority

Stewards MAY:
- Allocate credits based on documented contribution
- Adjust credit balances within defined rules
- Recommend distributions

Stewards MAY NOT:
- Change directive percentages
- Transfer credits between pools
- Allocate credits to themselves without oversight
- Create new credit classes

---

### 5.4 Steward Constraints

All steward actions must be:
- Logged
- Auditable
- Reversible by Managing Authority

Self-dealing is prohibited unless explicitly approved.

---

## 6. Operational Authority

### 6.1 Definition

Operational Authority governs day-to-day execution of tasks.

This includes:
- Team leads
- Project managers
- Task coordinators

Operational Authority does NOT include:
- Allocation power
- Distribution power
- Governance power

---

### 6.2 Relationship to Rewards

Operational Authority may:
- Validate task completion
- Recommend reward issuance
- Submit contribution reports

Final reward or credit issuance requires steward or managing approval.

---

## 7. Authority Delegation Rules

### 7.1 Delegation Must Be Explicit

No authority is implied.

Delegation must specify:
- Scope
- Duration
- Revocation conditions
- Audit requirements

---

### 7.2 Revocation

All delegated authority:
- Is revocable
- Reverts automatically upon role exit
- Cannot be transferred

Revocation does not require cause unless contractually stated.

---
