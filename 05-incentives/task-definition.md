# Task Definition & Validation Model

This document defines how work is proposed, performed, validated,
and rewarded within the organization.

The system is intentionally inspired by competitive problem-solving
(e.g., blockchain mining) but is legally and structurally distinct.

---

## 1. Core Principle

Tasks are NOT:
- Employment contracts
- Guaranteed compensation
- Investments
- Securities offerings

Tasks ARE:
- Open contribution opportunities
- Competitively solvable problems
- Discretionarily rewarded efforts

No participant is entitled to reward solely for participation.

---

## 2. Task Types

Tasks fall into one or more categories:

| Task Type | Description |
|---------|-------------|
| Problem-Solving | Finding a correct or optimal answer |
| Creative | Producing acceptable creative output |
| Operational | Completing defined operational work |
| Planning | Providing strategic or analytical input |
| Financial | Identifying or structuring financial support |

Each task specifies:
- Applicable directive pool
- Validation method
- Reward range (not guarantee)

---

## 3. Open Submission Model (Mining Analogy)

### 3.1 Open Participation

Unless restricted, tasks are:
- Publicly visible
- Open to multiple simultaneous submissions
- Non-exclusive

Multiple participants may work on the same task.

This mirrors competitive mining without exclusivity.

---

### 3.2 Submission Rules

Submissions must:
- Address the task definition
- Be submitted through approved channels
- Include required metadata (author, time, method)

Partial solutions may be submitted unless explicitly prohibited.

---

## 4. Validation Mechanisms

Each task defines one or more validation methods.

### 4.1 Manual Validation
- Human review by Operational Authority or Steward
- Subjective or qualitative judgment permitted
- Final authority rests with Managing Authority

---

### 4.2 Algorithmic Validation
- Deterministic checks
- Objective criteria
- Pass/fail or scored outcomes

Example:
- Correct answer
- Performance threshold
- Benchmark match

---

### 4.3 AI-Assisted Validation
- AI evaluates submissions against defined criteria
- AI output is advisory
- Human authority confirms or overrides

AI may never be the sole final authority.

---

### 4.4 Hybrid Validation
Multiple validation methods may be combined.

Example:
- Algorithmic pre-check
- AI scoring
- Human final approval

---

## 5. Winner Selection

### 5.1 First-Valid Submission
In tasks where correctness is binary:
- First validated correct submission may be selected

---

### 5.2 Best Submission
In tasks with qualitative judgment:
- Best submission may be selected after review window

---

### 5.3 Multiple Winners
Tasks may reward:
- Multiple contributors
- Partial solutions
- Collaborative outcomes

Winner selection rules must be declared upfront.

---

## 6. Reward Determination

### 6.1 Reward Types

Tasks may award:
- Reward Tokens
- Allocation Credit eligibility
- Recognition or priority
- Access or role consideration

Tasks may NOT award:
- Guaranteed cash
- Equity
- Transferable value

---

### 6.2 Reward Ranges (Critical)

Tasks define:
- Maximum possible reward
- Possible minimum (including zero)

Example:
- “Up to 500 Reward Tokens”
- “Eligible for allocation credit consideration”

This avoids guaranteed compensation.

---

### 6.3 Non-Winning Submissions

Non-winning submissions:
- May receive no reward
- May receive partial recognition
- Create no entitlement

Effort alone does not guarantee reward.

---

## 7. Authority Involvement

| Stage | Authority |
|----|----------|
| Task creation | Managing / Steward |
| Submission | Open participants |
| Validation | Operational / AI / Steward |
| Final approval | Managing Authority |
| Reward issuance | Steward / Managing |

No automated system may issue rewards without human authorization.
