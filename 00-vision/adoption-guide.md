# Adoption Guide – Constitutional Business Framework

This guide explains how a new business adopts the Constitutional
Governance & Incentive Framework.

It is designed to:
- minimize legal and operational friction
- make collaboration easy from day one
- scale from solo founder → multi-entity corporation
- be AI-driven in the future

The guide is divided into:
1. Required Setup Variables
2. Optional Configuration Variables
3. Step-by-Step Adoption Process
4. AI-Driven Setup Model (Forward-Looking)

---

## 1. Required Setup Variables (Minimal Viable Adoption)

Every adopting business must define the following variables.
These are the **minimum inputs** required to generate compliant documents.

### 1.1 Entity Basics

- `company_name`
- `jurisdiction_state` (U.S. state)
- `entity_type` (default: LLC)
- `founder_count`
- `initial_managing_authority` (name or role)

---

### 1.2 Business Intent

Answer in plain language:

- `primary_business_activity`
- `secondary_activities` (optional)
- `for_profit` (true / false)
- `expected_time_horizon` (short / long / indefinite)

---

### 1.3 Participation Model

- `will_accept_open_contributors` (yes / no)
- `will_hire_employees` (yes / no / later)
- `will_use_contractors` (yes / no)
- `will_offer_equity_initially` (yes / no)

---

### 1.4 Allocation Directives (Default or Custom)

Choose one:

- `use_default_allocation_directives` (yes / no)

If yes, defaults apply:

| Directive | Default |
|---------|--------|
| Financial Assistance | 30% |
| Labor & Operations | 50% |
| Founder Allocation | 10% |
| Planning & Strategy | 10% |

If no, define:
- `directive_names[]`
- `directive_percentages[]` (must total 100%)

---

### 1.5 Authority Model

- `managing_authority_model` (single / board)
- `initial_stewards` (optional list)
- `will_delegate_authority` (yes / no)

---

## 2. Optional Configuration Variables (Advanced)

These variables refine behavior but are not required to start.

### 2.1 Incentives

- `reward_token_classes[]`
- `reward_expiration_enabled` (yes / no)
- `reward_to_allocation_conversion_enabled` (yes / no)

---

### 2.2 Blockchain Usage

- `use_blockchain` (none / audit-only / internal-ledger)
- `onchain_anchor_frequency` (never / periodic / per-event)
- `public_chain` (if applicable)

---

### 2.3 Compliance Posture

- `states_of_operation[]`
- `international_expansion_planned` (yes / no)
- `profit_linked_tokens_planned` (yes / no)

If yes:
→ securities compliance mode required

---

### 2.4 Evolution Intent

- `intent_to_convert_to_corp` (yes / no / unsure)
- `target_conversion_trigger` (funding / scale / revenue / time)

---

## 3. Step-by-Step Adoption Process

### Step 1 — Define Variables
Complete Sections 1 and 2 above.

This can be done:
- manually
- via form
- via AI interview

---

### Step 2 — Generate Core Documents

Using the variables, generate:

- Operating Agreement (LLC)
- Authority Model
- Allocation Directives
- Amendment Rules
- Incentive & Task Framework
- Risk Analyses (SEC + Labor)

At this stage:
- no blockchain is required
- no tokens need to exist
- governance is human-driven

---

### Step 3 — Establish Authority Roles

- Assign Managing Authority
- Appoint initial Stewards (if any)
- Document delegation boundaries

No contributor receives authority by default.

---

### Step 4 — Activate Incentives (Optional)

If incentives are enabled:
- define task categories
- define reward policies
- enable validation workflows

Participation must remain optional and discretionary.

---

### Step 5 — Activate Blockchain (Optional)

If blockchain is enabled:
- deploy audit anchoring
- deploy internal ledgers (non-transferable)
- bind smart contracts to policy versions

Blockchain must mirror decisions, not replace them.

---

### Step 6 — Operate & Iterate

Operate the business normally:
- hire when needed
- contract when needed
- reward contributions where appropriate

All evolution must respect amendment rules.

---

### Step 7 — Convert (Optional)

When ready:
- convert LLC → C-Corp using Charter Mapping
- migrate authority to Board
- preserve allocation directives

No incentives or credits are invalidated.

---

## 4. AI-Driven Setup Model (Forward-Looking)

This framework is intentionally AI-compatible.

### 4.1 AI Interview Flow (Conceptual)

An AI may ask:

- “What problem does your business solve?”
- “Who contributes value today?”
- “Who should benefit long-term?”
- “Do you want open contributors?”
- “Do you plan to raise capital?”
- “Do you want profit-linked instruments?”

Responses populate setup variables.

---

### 4.2 AI Output Capabilities

Given validated inputs, an AI can:

- generate operating agreements
- generate authority matrices
- generate allocation tables
- generate incentive policies
- generate compliance warnings
- recommend defaults
- flag risk triggers

Final approval must always be human.

---

### 4.3 Guardrails for AI Use

AI must not:
- grant authority
- issue rewards
- modify allocations
- approve distributions
- override governance

AI assists; humans decide.

---

## 5. Adoption Profiles (Examples)

### Profile A — Solo Founder
- use defaults
- no contributors initially
- no blockchain
- low friction

### Profile B — Open Collaboration Startup
- enable tasks + rewards
- audit-only blockchain
- strong labor guardrails

### Profile C — Capital-Intensive Business
- early allocation directives
- planned corp conversion
- investor-aware structure

---

## 6. Summary

This framework is designed so that:
- adoption is simple
- complexity is optional
- compliance is baked in
- evolution is predictable
- trust is preserved

A business can start in a day
and still be institution-ready years later.
